Hiking Trails

Mt. Tamalpais

Located in Northern California, just North of San Francisco, lies one of the most beautiful mountains in all of California. Hiking trails are scattered throughout rolling hills, with views of the Pacific in all directions.

On a clear day, visitors can see the Farallon Islands 25 miles out to sea, the Marin County hills, San Francisco and the bay, hills and cities of the East Bay, and Mount Diablo. On rare occasions, the Sierra Nevada's snow-covered mountains can be seen 150 miles away.

You won't want to miss a sunset from the top of Mt. Tamalpais ridge. On a warm night, the fog builds up and looks like waves along the horizon. Truly a surreal sight to see.

Cerro Cabrillo Coastal Trail

Located in Morro Bay State Park along the central coast lies The Cerro Cabrillo trailhead. The total distance of the hike can vary from 2 to 4 miles and the difficulty is moderate to extreme. Although it can be strenuous at times, the view is completely worth it.

This hike is recommended for those looking to get a great workout and a bit of an adrenaline rush. So, if you're a thrill seeker, this one is for you! The highest point in the park is about 900 feet and is home to some amazing views. Beware of poison oak at the peak and be prepared to climb some boulders!

Bluff Trail

Montano de Oro is a perfect place to experience the breathtaking views the Central Coast has to offer. Located in the heart of this state park lies The Bluff Trail. a 2 mile trail takes you along the coast passing by wildflowers, steep bluffs, and hidden beaches.

A perfect place to take your loved ones for a day or evening outing. Easy access and a flat trail allows you to walk, run or even bike ride. Take a walk to the nearby beach, Spooners Cove to end your day of excersize.
